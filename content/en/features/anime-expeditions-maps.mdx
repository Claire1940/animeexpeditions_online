<Section icon={Swords} title="Raid Maps: Phase-by-Phase Layout Strategy">

Raid maps in Anime Expeditions offer some of the most dynamic challenges in the game. Rather than loading players into entirely different maps for each stage of a raid, the game utilizes a single, massive map layout. This layout is divided into three distinct zones, corresponding to Acts 1, 2, and 3 of the raid. As you progress through the acts, the starting spawn points and defensive lines shift deeper into the map, forcing you to adapt your strategy to entirely different terrain.

For example, in the Karakura Raid, Act 1 takes place at the outer gates, featuring a relatively simple path with wide defensive curves. Act 2 moves inside the city streets, introducing tighter corridors and faster enemy spawn rates. Act 3 takes place at the portal entrance, where you must face the boss on a layout with multiple intersecting lanes and environmental hazards.

| Raid Act | Map Area | Boss Unit | Key Mechanic | Recommended Unit Types |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Act 1** | Outer City Gates | Hollow Vanguard | Heavy physical shields | High-damage physical attackers |
| **Act 2** | Inner Karakura | Arrancar Elite | High-speed sprinters | Slow, freeze, and stun supports |
| **Act 3** | Soul Society Portal | Kinpachi Boss | Massive AoE stuns | Long-range magic and track units |

To consistently clear these multi-phase raid maps, follow this strategic step-by-step approach:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Set Up Your Economy Early">
    Place your farm units (such as Bulma or Speedwagon) in safe corners away from the main path. Use the auto-upgrade toggle keybind to maximize their yield during the quiet early waves.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Establish Chokepoint Controls">
    Identify the main intersections where paths merge. Place slowing or freezing units like Rukia at these junctions to bunch up enemy waves for your main damage dealers.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Deploy High-Yield DPS Summons">
    Once your economy is stable, deploy your primary damage dealers. Units with large circular or cone-shaped AoE attacks, such as Kinpachi, should be positioned to cover multiple segments of the path.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Adapt to Boss Abilities">
    When the raid boss emerges, monitor their movement and ability indicators. Relocate your support units or activate manual abilities to counter boss stuns and high-speed dashes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Special event maps are a cornerstone of Anime Expeditions, introducing unique gameplay mechanics that diverge from standard tower defense rules. The most notable of these is the Creator Spotlight series, where custom stages designed by prominent content creators are featured in limited-time rotations. These maps often require players to use specific units or adapt to unusual environmental rules.

In Sebi's Dress Rosa map, players are restricted to using Luffy units, but are introduced to a unique "Merge" mechanic. Placing a Luffy unit within the range of another Luffy merges them into a single, larger unit. This merged unit gains a 10% stat boost, increased size, and enhanced shield-breaking capabilities for every unit absorbed. This mechanic completely changes how you utilize the map's space, turning unit placement into a puzzle of spatial optimization.
