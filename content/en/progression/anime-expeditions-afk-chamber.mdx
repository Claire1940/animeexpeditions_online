<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Step-by-Step: Setting Up Your TinyTask Macro">

Automating this process requires a reliable macro recorder. Popular choices among the community include TinyTask and Informal Task. The goal is to record a perfect run, including unit placements, upgrade sequences, and the final retry loop, then set the macro to repeat infinitely while you are away from your device.

Before starting, ensure your Roblox graphics settings are set to low to prevent performance dips that could desynchronize your macro. Additionally, run the game in windowed mode with a fixed resolution to ensure mouse clicks land on the exact coordinates every time.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initiate the Recording">
    Open your macro tool (TinyTask or Informal Task), set your recording and playback hotkeys, and start the King's Tomb Mastery stage manually to ensure a clean starting state.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Deploy Economy Units Instantly">
    As soon as the stage loads, place your Stone Alchemist and Ramen Guy immediately. Prioritize placing the Stone Alchemist first, as their passive income scales faster in the early waves.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Place DPS and Configure Upgrades">
    Deploy your primary DPS units (Kenpachi and Frieren) near the enemy track. Set your economy units to auto-upgrade, then manually queue upgrades for your main damage dealers to boost their attack speed and damage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Record the Retry Sequence">
    Once the boss is defeated and the victory screen appears, spam-click the "Repeat Stage" or "Retry" button area for 10 seconds to account for any loading variance, then stop the recording and set it to loop.
  </Step>
</Steps>

When configuring the upgrade order, avoid setting auto-upgrades while units are still being placed. This ensures the macro does not attempt to upgrade a unit that has not yet been deployed, which can cause the recording to desync and ultimately fail the run.

<Callout type="info" title="Macro Timing">
Always add a 5 to 10-second buffer of spam-clicking the retry button at the end of your recording. This prevents the macro from breaking if you encounter minor network lag or longer loading screens.
</Callout>

</Section>
