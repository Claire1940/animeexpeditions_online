<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Campaign Progression Walkthrough">

Progression in Anime Expeditions is structured around completing campaign maps, which unlocks higher difficulty modes, better equipment, and advanced game features. The campaign maps are streamlined into 5 acts per map, unlike older tower defense games that require 6 acts. This makes the progression loop much faster and more rewarding.

To progress efficiently, you should optimize your game settings. Features like Auto-Skip, Auto-Retry, and Auto-Next are incredibly useful for grinding materials and experience points.

| Act / Stage | Difficulty | Key Enemies | Recommended Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **School Grounds Act 1-5** | Normal | Standard Hollows | Level 1-5 |
| **School Grounds Act 1** | Hard | Boss (6K Health) | Level 5+ |
| **Flower Forest Act 1-5** | Normal | Forest Spirits | Level 8-10 |

To conquer these stages with ease, follow this structured progression route.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Optimize Your Settings">
    Enable Wave Skip and Auto-Skip in the settings menu. Only disable Wave Skip if you are severely struggling on a specific boss wave.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Complete School Grounds Normal">
    Run through Acts 1 to 5 of the School Grounds map. This unlocks your basic equipment slots and provides starter gems.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Clear Act 1 Hard Mode">
    Switch the difficulty to Hard and complete Act 1. You only need to do this once to satisfy progression quests, then switch back to Normal for Act 2.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Push to Flower Forest">
    Complete the Flower Forest map on Normal. Reaching this milestone unlocks the fourth unit slot on your active loadout.
  </Step>
</Steps>

A critical combat mechanic to master is **Ghost Placement**. When placing a unit, you can position them as a semi-transparent "ghost" even if you do not have enough cash. The game will automatically construct the unit the exact millisecond your wallet reaches the required amount, preventing leaks on fast-paced waves.

<Callout type="tip" title="Economy Tip">
Get into the habit of selling all your economy units on Wave 15. Since it is the final wave, you no longer need passive income, and the extra cash can be used to max out your primary DPS units for the boss fight.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Evolution, Crafting, and Stat Rolling">

As you transition into the mid-game, raw levels are no longer enough to carry your team. You must utilize the Upgrade Zone to evolve your units, craft rare materials, and roll powerful traits. Evolving a unit completely changes their skill set, often adding massive area-of-effect attacks, status ailments, or utility buffs.

To evolve a unit, you need specific evolution materials (essence) and a unique character item. For example, evolving **Yuta** requires various rarities of essence alongside his **Student ID**, which is obtained by completing his specific Unit Trial.

| Material Name | Rarity | Base Crafting Cost | Total Needed (Evo) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

| **Purple Essence** | Epic | 5 Blue Essence | 20 |
| **Rainbow Essence** | Legendary | Found in Legend Stages | 5 |
