<Section icon={Map} title="Core Placement Mechanics & Lane Geometry">

To conquer the challenging waves and formidable bosses in this Roblox anime tower defense adventure, mastering unit positioning is your key to victory. Ahead of the full release on July 17, 2026, understanding how to position your anime-inspired units can mean the difference between clearing a difficult Raid and leaking fast enemies. 

In any tower defense game, map geometry dictates defense efficiency. Placing units blindly along a straight path is a common mistake that wastes valuable range. Instead, players must focus on "Time on Target" (ToT)—the total duration an enemy remains within a unit's attack radius. 

The most effective spots on any map are corners, U-bends, and chokepoints where lanes overlap. A unit placed inside a U-bend can attack enemies as they approach, as they loop around, and as they retreat, effectively tripling its damage output compared to a straight-lane placement.

| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

| **Corners & Bends** | ★★★★★ | AoE, High-Range DPS | Maximizes time enemies spend within the unit attack radius |
| **Overlapping Lanes** | ★★★★★ | Support Buffers, Main Carries | Buffs multiple defense zones, covers split-path rushes |
| **Exit/Backline** | ★★★☆☆ | Single-Target Finishers | Catches leaking fast runners, secures low-health boss kills |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
Avoid placing your strongest damage dealers directly at the map entrance. If fast-moving enemies rush past them before their attack animations finish, your defense will leak. Keep your high-damage units slightly further back where crowd control units have already slowed the enemy advance.

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Defense Setup">

A successful match requires a structured deployment strategy. Placing expensive units too early will leave you defenseless against fast early-game waves, while over-deploying cheap units will cause you to run out of placement slots during boss phases. Follow this step-by-step progression to manage your resources and layout effectively.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Deploy a Cheap Opener">
    Start the match by placing a cost-effective unit near the entrance or the first major bend. Excellent starters include Epic units like **Nen Hunter** or your beginner exclusive units. This holds off the first few waves while you accumulate Yen.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Establish Crowd Control">
    Before the first fast-moving runner wave arrives, place a crowd control unit like **Salmon Sorcerer** near the entrance. This slows the enemy advance by 20% and groups them together, preparing them for your area-of-effect damage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Position Your Main Carry">
    Identify the premier corner on the map and deploy your primary damage dealer, such as **Cursed Student** or **Shadow**. This unit will be the anchor of your entire defense, handling high-health targets and boss waves.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Upgrade Before Placing More">
    Avoid the temptation to fill every placement slot with unupgraded units. In Anime Expeditions, a single fully upgraded unit is significantly stronger than five low-level units. Concentrate your Yen on upgrading your main carry to unlock their evolved skill sets.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Deploy Support Buffers">
    Once your main carry is highly upgraded, place support units like **The Hero** nearby. Ensure their buffing auras overlap with your damage dealers to amplify their damage output for the final boss waves.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Trait Reroll Advice">

Anime Expeditions features several distinct game modes, each requiring a tailored positioning strategy. A layout that clears Story Mode easily might fail completely in cooperative Raids or objective-based Expeditions.

However, once you reach account level 20 and unlock **Expeditions & Payload**, the objective shifts. You must protect a moving payload while managing resources. For **Raids** (unlocked at level 25), the focus shifts entirely to boss melting and crowd management. Raid bosses possess massive health pools and can disable your units. You must build a highly coordinated layout with clustered support buffs and dedicated single-target boss killers.
