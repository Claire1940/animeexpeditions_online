<Section icon={Swords} title="Advanced Unit Upgrades: Traits and Stat Potential">

Summoning the unit is only the first step. To compete in high-level raids and tournaments, you must optimize their **Traits** and **Stat Potential**. The upgrade section in the lobby houses the Trait Rerolling station and the Stat Potential altar. Traits function similarly to other tower defense games but with a few unique twists in the Anime Expeditions meta.

| Trait Name | Rarity | Main Effect | Meta Rating |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Unique/Monarch** | Mythic | 1 Placement Limit / Massive Stat Boost | ★★★★★ |
| **Primordial** | Mythic | Divine Damage Multipliers | ★★★★★ |
| **Crit Trait** | Mythic | Significant Crit Rate & Damage Increase | ★★★★☆ |
| **DOT Trait** | Mythic | Burn/Poison Effects + Lower Unit Cost | ★★★★☆ |
| **Money Trait** | Epic | Increased Wave Income | ★★★☆☆ |

Stat Potential determines the base quality of your unit's attributes, ranging from D to **Z (Godly)**. The system features a **Worthiness Bar** that fills as you roll. A key feature here is that the meter can overfill; any extra progress is carried over to the next bar, ensuring no resources are wasted during the quest for Triple-S or Z-rank stats.

<Section icon={Target} title="Evolution and Unit Trials">

Evolution in Anime Expeditions moves away from the traditional "grind the same stage for 20 hours" model. Instead, it utilizes **Unit Trials**. Once you pull a specific Mythic, a unique quest appears in your quest log. These trials provide a preset team, challenging your tactical skill rather than your raw unit power.

Evolved units gain powerful new passives. For example, the **Salmon Sorcerer** gains a support stun ability upon evolution, making him viable for late-game crowd control. Furthermore, evolution unlocks an additional equipment slot specifically for unit-unique items found in **Mastery Stages**.

<Callout type="info" title="Mastery Stages">
After completing all five floors of a story map, you unlock **Mastery Mode**. This mode has a 5% drop rate (with a 20% pity) for unique unit equipment. These items act like a second passive, further specializing your character's role in the team.
</Callout>
